Freelancing was supposed to be fun

Instead, it feels like a slog. You're still paycheck to paycheck, except now that paycheck is paid by freaking net-30. Your client (singular) treats you like an employee, and your dry spells are scary. It's enough to send you back to that job you hated.

You can have freedom, money, respect. You can run the show, and say no.

As a future consultant, the end goal is to get the same respect for your time, education, training, and skills as that business owner has for their surgeon.

With respect, you’ll run the show. You’ll name your rates and the clients will say “OK”. You’ll choose projects that make sense for your long term vision and short term cashflow.

And you won’t be chasing invoices, pleading for payments, or taking crap projects.

Consultants fix problems. Freelancers are hired. You should choose to be a consultant.

In this book, you'll learn ways to earn, keep, and use respect to your advantage.

Employ this workflow, and the respect will flow, followed by increased rates and full schedules.
